## The Contemporary World Principle

This is the soul of our cultural approach. It governs every story, every illustration, every character.

### The Rule

> **Show cultures as they ARE today, not as the West imagines them.**

The world is largely modernised. A child in Doha, Tokyo, Bangkok, Nairobi, or São Paulo has far more in common with a child in London than most children's books suggest. They wear similar clothes. They go to similar schools. They live in modern homes. They play similar games.

**Tradition is the seasoning, not the main dish.** Cultural identity comes through in:

- The food on the table
- The pattern on a fabric
- The shape of a building
- The sounds in the street
- A festival, a gathering, a custom woven into daily life

But the baseline is **modern, comfortable, dignified daily life** — because that is what life actually looks like for the vast majority of the world's children.

### What We Avoid

| ❌ Postcard Stereotype | ✅ Contemporary Reality |
|---|---|
| Japan = kimonos, zen gardens, pagodas | Japan = casual clothes, train platforms, modern neighbourhoods with cherry blossoms lining the street |
| Africa = mud huts, safari, poverty | Africa = modern cities, apartments, schools, colourful textiles in everyday fashion |
| Middle East = desert, camels, souks only | Middle East = glass towers, green parks, modern and traditional dress side by side |
| South Asia = overcrowded, saris, rickshaws | South Asia = modern apartments, family cars, vibrant festivals woven into modern life |
| Caribbean = beach huts, poverty | Caribbean = colourful modern homes, community parks, tropical gardens, music culture |

### The Acid Test

> **Could a child FROM this culture see themselves in this book?**

A Japanese child should look at our Japan book and think *"that looks like my life"* — not *"that looks like a museum"*.

## What Makes Us Different

We have four pillars. Every book must pass all four.

### 1. 🔤 Phonics Accuracy
Every word in every story is either **decodable** at the target reading level or a **listed tricky word**. No exceptions. This is the educational core — it is what makes our books *work* for learning to read.

### 2. 📖 Narrative Engagement
Every story has a **clear emotional journey** — problem, tension, satisfying resolution. We use Dear Zoo-style engagement hooks: page-turn cliffhangers, curiosity gaps, repetition with variation. Phonics compliance is not enough; the story must make children **want to turn the page**.

### 3. 🎨 Visual Consistency
Every book uses a **hero injection pipeline** for character consistency. One character reference image is generated once, then mechanically injected into every scene. This ensures the same face, outfit, proportions, and art style across every page. Objects that recur are defined once and depicted identically in every appearance.

### 4. 🌍 Cultural Authenticity
Every non-British book begins with **mandatory cultural research** (Step 0 of our pipeline). A Cultural Brief is produced before any story is written or any illustration is designed. The brief ensures:

- Authentic, verified details (clothing, architecture, food, landscape)
- Contemporary-first approach (how people live TODAY)
- No stereotypes, no cultural mixing, no poverty tourism
- Internal consistency (all details from the SAME region)
- Dignity check (would someone from this culture feel proud?)

## The 9-Step Production Pipeline

Every book is made through a mandatory 9-step pipeline with quality checkpoints:

```
STEP 0   Cultural Research     → Verified Cultural Brief
STEP 1   Story Writing         → Phonics-compliant, engaging story
STEP 2   Story QA              → Phonics + engagement + narrative checks
STEP 2b  Character Selection   → Outfit suits story + culture
STEP 3a  Object Identification → Recurring objects defined once
STEP 3b  Image Prompts         → Hero + scene prompts with cultural details
STEP 4   Image Generation      → Hero injection pipeline (Gemini/Flux)
STEP 5   Image QA              → Every image read and verified
STEP 6   Book Assembly         → PDF via Playwright
STEP 7   Final QA              → All 9 checks + vibe check
```

**Cultural research is Step 0** — it happens BEFORE anything else. The Cultural Brief then guides every subsequent step: the story writer uses it for correct terms and customs, the illustration director uses it for accurate clothing and architecture, and the book assessor verifies against it at final QA.
