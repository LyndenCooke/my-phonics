## Core Knowledge Base

### Read Write Inc (RWI) — Ruth Miskin / Oxford University Press

**Set 1 Single-Letter Sounds (Speed Sounds)** — taught in this exact order:
1. m, a, s, d, t
2. i, n, p, g, o
3. c, k, u, b
4. f, e, l, h
5. r, j, v, y, w
6. z, x

**Set 1 Special Friends (digraphs):**
- sh, th, ch, qu, ng, nk
- Also: ff, ll, ss, zz, ck (double consonants taught as "best friends")

**Set 2 Sounds (long vowel sounds — 12 total):**
- ay (day), ee (tree), igh (high), ow (blow)
- oo (zoo), oo (look), ar (car), or (for)
- air (fair), ir (bird), ou (shout), oy (boy)

**Set 3 Sounds (alternative spellings — 19 total):**
- ea (tea), oi (coin), a-e (cake), i-e (kite)
- o-e (home), u-e (cute), aw (saw), are (share)
- ur (nurse), er (her), ow (cow), ai (rain)
- oa (boat), ew (new), ire (fire), ear (hear)
- ure (sure), tion (station), cious/tious (precious)

**RWI Word Types:**
- **Green Words** = fully decodable using taught graphemes
- **Red Words** = exception/tricky words (common but not fully decodable)
- **Nonsense/Alien Words** = pseudo-words for pure decoding assessment

**RWI Assessment Structure:**
- Sound assessment: child reads individual sounds from a grid
- Word reading: green words, red words, nonsense words
- Fluency: timed reading of connected text
- Colour-banded book levels: Ditty → Red → Green → Purple → Pink → Orange → Yellow → Blue → Grey

**RWI Storybook Progression:**
- Ditty books: CVC words only (Set 1 sounds)
- Red books: Set 1 sounds + Special Friends
- Green books: Set 2 sounds introduced
- Purple onwards: Set 3 sounds introduced
- Each level introduces ~2-4 new graphemes

### Letters and Sounds (DfE 2007)

**Phase 1:** Environmental sounds, rhythm, rhyme, alliteration (pre-reading)

**Phase 2 (Reception, first 6 weeks):**
- Week 1: s, a, t, p
- Week 2: i, n, m, d
- Week 3: g, o, c, k
- Week 4: ck, e, u, r
- Week 5: h, b, f, ff, l, ll, ss
- Week 6: consolidation
- Tricky words: the, to, I, no, go, into

**Phase 3 (Reception, 12 weeks):**
- Set 6: j, v, w, x
- Set 7: y, z, zz, qu
- Consonant digraphs: ch, sh, th, ng
- Vowel digraphs: ai, ee, igh, oa, oo (long), oo (short), ar, or, ur, ow, oi, ear, air, ure, er
- Tricky words: he, she, we, me, be, was, you, they, all, are, my, her

**Phase 4 (Reception-Y1, 4-6 weeks):**
- NO new graphemes
- Focus: consonant clusters (CCVC and CVCC words)
- Examples: frog, blog, stop, clap, tent, damp, toast, bench
- Tricky words: said, have, like, so, do, some, come, were, there, little, one, when, out, what

**Phase 5 (Year 1, full year):**
- Alternative spellings: ay, ou, ie, ea, oy, ir, ue, aw, wh, ph, ew, oe, au, ey, a-e, e-e, i-e, o-e, u-e
- Alternative pronunciations: i (fin/find), o (hot/cold), c (cat/city), g (got/giant), u (but/put), ow (cow/blow), ie (tie/field), ea (eat/bread), er (her/farmer), a (hat/what/acorn), y (yes/by/very), ch (chin/school/chef), ou (out/shoulder/could/you)
- Tricky words: oh, their, people, Mr, Mrs, looked, called, asked, could

**Phase 6 (Year 2, full year):**
- Suffixes: -s, -es, -ing, -ed, -er, -est, -ly, -ment, -ness, -ful, -less, -ly
- Prefixes: un-, dis-, re-, pre-
- Spelling rules, syllable division, past tense patterns

### UK Phonics Screening Check (PSC)

- **When:** End of Year 1 (age 5-6), statutory since 2012
- **Format:** 40 words total — 20 real words + 20 pseudo-words (nonsense words)
- **Structure:** Section 1 (simpler GPCs from Phase 2-3), Section 2 (more complex GPCs from Phase 3-5)
- **Pass mark:** 32/40 (varies slightly by year)
- **What it tests:** Grapheme-phoneme correspondence (GPC) knowledge and blending ability
- **Pseudo-words:** Presented with an alien character picture so children know they're not real words
- **Re-check:** Children who don't meet threshold retake in Year 2

- Group 1: s, a, t, i, p, n

- Aligns closely with Letters and Sounds phases
- Stage 1+ = Phase 2, Stage 2 = Phase 3, Stage 3 = Phase 4, etc.

**Essential Letters and Sounds (ELS):**
- Updated version of Letters and Sounds
- DfE validated, used by many schools since 2021
- Same phase structure but with revised word lists and decodable readers

**Little Wandle Letters and Sounds Revised:**
- Most widely adopted DfE-validated SSP programme (2021+)
- Revised Letters and Sounds with explicit teaching sequences
- Includes structured assessment checkpoints

## How to Use This Knowledge

When asked about phonics curriculum, progression, or assessment:

1. **Always reference the specific programme** being discussed (RWI, Letters and Sounds, etc.)
2. **Be precise about sound order** — the teaching sequence matters enormously
3. **Distinguish between graphemes and phonemes** — graphemes are written, phonemes are spoken
4. **Know the terminology differences:**
   - RWI says "green words" / "red words" / "alien words"
   - Letters and Sounds says "decodable words" / "tricky words" / "pseudo-words"
   - MyPhonicsBooks says "story words" / "tricky words" / "nonsense words"
5. **Understand cumulative progression** — each level only uses sounds taught so far
6. **Apply the "no guessing" principle** — SSP is about decoding, not memorising whole words
7. **Know assessment boundaries** — what sounds/words are tested at each checkpoint

## Key Pedagogical Principles

1. **Systematic** — sounds taught in a fixed, researched order
2. **Synthetic** — children synthesise (blend) individual sounds into words
3. **Cumulative** — each new step builds on previously taught sounds
4. **Decodable text** — children only read words made from sounds they've been taught
5. **No guessing** — pictures support comprehension, not word identification
6. **Pace** — new sounds introduced at roughly 4 per week in early stages
7. **Revisit and review** — previously taught sounds practised daily
8. **Assessment-informed** — regular checks determine which sounds need re-teaching
